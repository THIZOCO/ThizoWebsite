2025: Advanced Building Materials That Commercial Interior Designers Love


When it comes to designing commercial interiors, whether for healthcare, education, office spaces, multi-family housing, or retail, it’s not just about aesthetics anymore. Advanced materials are revolutionizing the way we think about functionality, sustainability, and even comfort. Here are eight standout materials—and the manufacturers behind them—that every commercial architect, designer, or engineer in North America should have on their radar.


Acoustic Panels by Ecophon: Silence, Please


For open office layouts and busy retail spaces, noise can be a major disruptor. Enter Ecophon’s acoustic panels. Made from recycled glass wool, these panels absorb sound efficiently while offering sleek, customizable designs. Perfect for creating a serene environment in otherwise chaotic settings.


Why Specify It? High acoustic performance meets sustainability. Source: Ecophon


Anti-Microbial Surfaces by Corian®: Healthcare’s Best Friend


Hygiene is critical in healthcare and multi-family housing, and Corian® has stepped up with its anti-microbial solid surfaces. Resistant to bacteria and easy to clean, these surfaces are ideal for countertops, walls, and workstations. They’re functional, durable, and undeniably sleek.


Why Specify It? Built-in hygiene with aesthetic appeal. Source: Corian® Design


Smart Glass by View: Adaptive Transparency


Natural light is a designer’s best friend, but glare and heat can be challenging. View’s smart glass solves this with its ability to adjust tint automatically based on sunlight. Ideal for retail and office spaces, it reduces energy costs while creating a comfortable atmosphere.


Why Specify It? Energy efficiency and dynamic aesthetics. Source: View Smart Glass


Biophilic Wall Panels by Moss Walls: Bringing Nature Indoors


For spaces craving a touch of green, Moss Walls delivers with its biophilic wall panels. These low-maintenance, natural moss installations enhance air quality and bring a calming, organic vibe to interiors, perfect for healthcare or multi-family common areas.


Why Specify It? Sustainable design with a natural touch. Source: Moss Walls


Luxury Vinyl Tile (LVT) by Armstrong Flooring: Durable Elegance


For high-traffic areas like retail or education spaces, Armstrong Flooring’s luxury vinyl tile is a game-changer. It’s incredibly durable, comes in a variety of finishes, and mimics the look of natural wood or stone without the maintenance hassles.


Why Specify It? Affordable luxury with commercial-grade durability. Source: Armstrong Flooring


Fire-Resistant Panels by Trespa®: Safety First


Safety is non-negotiable in commercial design. Trespa®’s fire-resistant panels are perfect for educational and healthcare facilities, providing a combination of safety, durability, and aesthetic flexibility. Plus, they’re available in a range of colors and finishes.


Why Specify It? Aesthetic safety for high-risk areas. Source: Trespa®


Recycled Carpet Tiles by Interface®: Walk the Sustainability Talk


Sustainability meets style with Interface®’s recycled carpet tiles. These modular flooring solutions are made from reclaimed materials and designed to reduce waste. They’re perfect for offices or education spaces where durability and design go hand in hand.


Why Specify It? Eco-friendly, modular, and easy to install. Source: Interface®


LED Architectural Lighting by Lumenpulse: Light the Way


Lumenpulse specializes in high-performance LED lighting systems that are as energy-efficient as they are visually stunning. Ideal for retail and office spaces, their systems allow for customizable lighting schemes to suit any design vision.


Why Specify It? Energy savings with endless design possibilities. Source: Lumenpulse


Key Trends These Materials Address


Sustainability: Recycled carpet tiles, moss walls, and acoustic panels align with eco-conscious design priorities. Hygiene: Anti-microbial surfaces meet the needs of healthcare and multi-family housing. Comfort: Acoustic and biophilic designs enhance user well-being. Energy Efficiency: Smart glass and LED lighting reduce operational costs and environmental impact. Safety: Fire-resistant panels prioritize occupant safety without sacrificing style. Durability: Luxury vinyl tile offers resilience for high-traffic areas. Innovation: Smart materials and adaptive technologies redefine interior spaces. Aesthetics: From moss walls to dynamic lighting, these materials offer design flexibility without compromise.


Why It Matters


Advanced materials for commercial interiors aren’t just about ticking boxes on a spec sheet. They’re about creating spaces where people thrive, whether that’s in a bustling retail environment, a healing healthcare facility, or a collaborative office. With these eight materials, you’re not just designing interiors; you’re designing experiences.


So, the next time you’re working on a commercial project, consider these innovations. They’re sustainable, stylish, and ready to take your designs to the next level.
